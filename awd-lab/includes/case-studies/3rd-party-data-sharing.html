Privacy & Data interface

Case Study

Adapted a healthcare privacy interface when requirements shifted from global data sharing to app-level permissions.

Overview

The initial design assumed global data sharing. As the product evolved, the requirements shifted to per-application permissions. I redesigned the experience to support app-level visibility and control while keeping required legal disclosures visible on the page.

    Key Considerations

  • Legal data-sharing compliance requirements shifted during the design phase
  • The initial design solution could not support the new set of constraints
  • I designed an interface where disclosures, status, and actions could be quickly scanned

Project Summary

Context & Challenge

Members needed a clear way to see which third-party apps were accessing their protected health information, understand each app’s status, and stop sharing when needed. The page also had to include a large amount of required legal copy above the table, keep all actions on a single page, and use a legacy UI kit. I simplified this compliance-heavy experience while keeping the interface easy to scan and act on.

Design Constraints

  • Limited design and development budget
  • Legal copy must remain visible at all times
  • Scan, review, and modify app-sharing data with minimal actions
Result

Redesigned a simplified interface into a structured solution where member and app statuses could be scanned, updated, and managed while maintaining required legal disclosures.

Reflection

This project reflects how I often worked at Florida Blue. While my role focused on visual design, I frequently collaborated with interaction designers, product managers, and engineers to translate complex requirements into clear interface patterns. On this effort, I used hierarchy, spacing, status indicators, and structured content to present sensitive data-sharing information clearly while supporting the business and compliance requirements behind the feature.

“Strong visual design is not decoration. In regulated product spaces, it becomes the structure that helps users understand risk, trust the system, and take action with confidence.”
